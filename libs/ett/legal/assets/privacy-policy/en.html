Introduction

As the data controller, data protection is very important to Energinet DataHub A/S (hereinafter Energinet DataHub). We safeguard the personal data we handle, and we ensure that we comply with data protection legislation.

We provide the persons about whom we process information about ('data subjects') with information about our data processing and about their rights as data subjects.

When you use Energy Track & Trace DK, we collect and process a range of information about you.

In this privacy policy, you can read about the purpose of the processing, what information we collect, and how we process the information.

Who we are – and how you can contact us

Energinet DataHub is a subsidiary of Energinet, which manages the operation of both Energy Track & Trace DK, ElOverblik, and the DataHub system. Energinet is an independent public company under the Ministry of Climate, Energy and Supply. Our task is to own and develop the electricity and gas grid in Denmark in order to adapt to a more sustainable energy, maintain security of supply and ensure equal market access to the electricity and gas grid.

Purpose of processing personal data

When we process information about you in Energy Track & Trace DK, it is for the purpose of giving you access to your own data. Additionally, Energy Track & Trace DK also serves the purpose of providing you as a producer or consumer with access to the issuance and use of granular certificates to document that the electricity is green at the time it is used. Furthermore, you have the option to grant powers of attorney to so-called third parties, such as energy advisors, brokers, or other market participants, who can act on your behalf regarding the issuance and use of granular certificates.

Legal basis for the processing of personal data

Energinet DataHub only processes your personal data to the extent necessary to fulfill the above purposes and only in accordance with applicable law. The legal basis for processing your information in Energy Track & Trace DK is Article 6(1)(b) of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The processing of personal data in Energy Track & Trace DK is therefore necessary for the performance of a contract.

We do not use automated decision-making, including profiling, as part of our processing of personal data.

Your Rights

You have several rights under the law in relation to our processing of personal data about you. You can make use of your personal data rights by contacting us. You will find our contact information at the top of this Privacy Policy.

Once you have requested access to the personal data, we have registered about you, to have it corrected or deleted, or if you have objected to our data processing, we will investigate whether it is possible to accommodate your request. We will respond to your inquiry as soon as possible and no later than one month after we have received your inquiry.

  • Right of access
    You have the right to access the information we process about you.
  • Right to rectification
    If you believe that the personal data we process about you is inaccurate, you have the right to have it corrected. You must contact us and inform us of the inaccuracies and what they should be corrected to.
  • Right to erasure or "the right to be forgotten"
    We delete personal data when it is no longer necessary to retain it. In special cases, you have the right to have specific information about you deleted before our regular deletion occurs. If you no longer wish to be registered in Energy Track & Trace DK, you can delete your information at any time via Energy-trackandtrace.dk, provided it is not required to be retained by law.
  • Right to restriction of processing
    If you disagree with the information we have registered or otherwise process, you can request that we restrict the processing of the information until we have had the opportunity to determine whether the information is correct. You can also request restriction instead of deletion if you believe our processing of the information is unlawful, or if you believe we no longer need the information, or if you believe that your legitimate interests outweigh Energinet's legitimate interests as the data controller.
  • Right to data portability
    You have the right, in certain cases, to receive your personal data and to request that the personal data be transferred from one data controller to another.
  • Right to object
    You have the right to object to our processing of information about you. If your objection is justified, we will ensure that the processing of the information ceases.
  • Right to complain
    If you are not satisfied with our response, you can complain to the Data Protection Agency. Generally, if you are dissatisfied with the way your personal data has been handled, you can complain to the Data Protection Agency, which will then investigate the matter and make a decision. You can find the contact details of the Data Protection Agency at https://www.datatilsynet.dk/english
